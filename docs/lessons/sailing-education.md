# Sailing Education Plan

## ASA Certification Progression

| Course | Title | Description | Priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| ASA 101 | Basic Keelboat | Skipper a 20-27' sloop by day, light-moderate winds. Terminology, helm commands, basic sail trim, points of sail, collision avoidance. | **Start here** |
| ASA 103 | Coastal Cruising | Skipper an auxiliary-powered 25-35' keelboat by day. Boat systems, engine operation, docking, intermediate sail trim, coastal navigation, anchoring, weather. | **Core** |
| ASA 104 | Bareboat Cruising | Multi-day cruise on a 30-45' sailboat in moderate-heavy winds. Provisioning, advanced systems, advanced sail trim, coastal nav, emergency ops. | **Core** |
| ASA 105 | Coastal Navigation | Theory and practice for coastal/inland navigation. Home study course — work through on own schedule. | **Important** |
| ASA 106 | Advanced Coastal Cruising | Skipper a 30-50' vessel in any conditions. Heavy weather, night sailing, all-conditions seamanship. | **Goal** |
| ASA 118 | Docking Endorsement | Master docking with confidence and precision. Critical for singlehanded slip entry. | **Must-have** |

## Why Start at 101

Even with previous boat ownership experience:
- Previous experience was informal and ended with a grounding
- 101 builds a clean, rigorous foundation without bad habits carried forward
- Shared vocabulary and idioms from 101 carry through all subsequent courses
- When an instructor says "bear away" in 25 knots during ASA 106, the response needs to be automatic
- Quick confidence builder — already know more than you think

## Recommended School: Harbor Sailboats

**Location**: 2040 Harbor Island Drive, San Diego, CA 92101

### Why Harbor Sailboats
- **Club + school model**: After certification, rent boats and keep sailing regularly during boat search period
- **Full progression**: ASA 101 through 106 at one school with instructors who know your development
- **Fleet**: 30+ boats (Beneteau, Catalina, Lagoon) from 22' to 40'+; learn on similar boats to what you'll buy
- **Community**: Club environment connects you with other sailors, potential crew, local knowledge
- **Location**: Harbor Island puts you in the marina/boatyard ecosystem
- **Track record**: Founded 1969, ASA School of the Year winner, all staff USCG Licensed + ASA Certified
- **Accountability**: Monthly sailing requirement + qualification maintenance policy

### Membership Terms
- One-year commitment required
- Members must sail every month
- Club reserves right to require qualification sail if skills lapse
- 100% of membership applied toward sailing
- **Waiting until employment location is confirmed before joining**

### Additional Offerings
- Flotillas (group sailing trips — Croatia, etc.)
- Racing courses
- Spinnaker courses
- Sail trim courses
- International Proficiency Certificate (for Mediterranean chartering)

### Alternatives Considered
- **Learn to Sail San Diego** — Six-time ASA award winner, private instruction, tailored to goals. Better for focused private lessons but less community/ongoing access.
- **Seaforth** — Four-day liveaboard course to Catalina. Great single experience.
- **J/World** — Five-day liveaboard course to Channel Islands. Prestigious but pricier.
- **Harbor Yacht Clubs** — US Sailing (not ASA) certification, 60+ boat fleet, membership includes free Basic Keelboat course.

## Suggested Timeline

### Immediate (pre-employment confirmation)
- Research schools, visit Harbor Sailboats in person
- Read ASA 101 and 105 textbooks
- Walk marina docks, talk to liveaboards and sailors

### After employment confirmed in San Diego
- Join Harbor Sailboats
- ASA 101 (first available date)
- ASA 103 (within 1-2 months of 101)
- ASA 118 Docking Endorsement (as soon as available)

### Ongoing
- ASA 104 Bareboat Cruising (multi-day liveaboard course — preview of liveaboard life)
- ASA 105 Coastal Navigation (home study, concurrent with other courses)
- Regular club boat rentals to build hours and confidence
- ASA 106 Advanced Coastal Cruising (when ready — covers heavy weather and night sailing)

## Tips for Instructors

Tell your instructor about:
- Previous boat ownership, the grounding off Point Loma, kelp fouling experience
- Liveaboard plan and ownership timeline
- Priority on systems, docking, anchoring, and judgment over recreational sailing
- Desire for rigor and solid fundamentals
- ADD tendencies — benefit from structured, repeatable frameworks

## Skills to Practice Beyond ASA

- **Docking under sail** — old-school skill, ultimate backup for engine failure
- **Heaving to** — critical heavy weather and rest technique
- **Reefing quickly** — practice until it's automatic
- **Man overboard recovery** — drill regularly
- **Night sailing** — covered in ASA 106 but needs extra practice
- **Anchoring in various conditions** — wind, current, tight anchorages

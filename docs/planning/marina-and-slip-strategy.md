# Marina & Slip Strategy — San Diego

## The Core Problem

Slips in San Diego are scarce. Shelter Island and America's Cup Harbor are generally waitlisted. Harbor Island has limited availability. South Bay has the most availability. This scarcity means the slip is the scarce asset, not the boat.

**Lesson learned the hard way**: Never buy a boat without confirmed moorage. The previous boat was lost because moorage ran out before a permanent solution was found.

## Marina Availability by Area

### Premium (longest waits, highest cost, best location)
- **Shelter Island** — Generally waitlisted. $40-50+/ft/month.
- **America's Cup Harbor** — Generally waitlisted.
- **Kona Kai** — $28-60/ft depending on slip. Screens boats on condition and value; requires photos before application.

### Mid-Range (some availability, moderate cost)
- **Harbor Island** — Limited availability. Harbor Island West, Marina Cortez (~500 slips).
- **Marina Village** — Mission Bay, 600+ slips, multiple sizes starting at 25'×11'. Good community reputation.

### South Bay (best availability, most affordable)
- **Pier 32 Marina** (National City) — ~$28/ft. Good amenities, fuel access, security.
- **Chula Vista Marina** — Generally more available.
- **South Bay marinas** — Further from open ocean (longer motor to sailing water), but more realistic for getting a slip.

### Tradeoff
South Bay = easier to get in, cheaper, longer commute to open water.
Shelter Island = best sailing access, hardest to get, most expensive.

## Liveaboard Permits

Liveaboard status is a **separate permission** on top of the slip rental, often with its own waiting list. Not all marinas allow liveaboards.

- Liveaboard fees: $200-$800/month on top of slip fees (single person: ~$350/month typical)
- Marinas cap the percentage of liveaboard slips (city ordinances, insurance)
- **Liveaboard permission is harder to get than the slip itself** — prioritize marinas that explicitly offer it

### Shelter Cove Marina Liveaboard Process
- Submit slip rental application with $25 waitlist fee
- Attach current color photo of vessel
- Include letter to marina manager requesting liveaboard privilege
- State: number of people, ages, occupation, readiness date
- Fees: $350/first person, $175/second, $100/third — on top of regular slip
- Limited pet policy, case-by-case basis

## Slip Acquisition Tactics

### Get on waitlists NOW
- Multiple lists simultaneously — even before owning a boat
- Small fee ($25-$50 typically), no boat ownership requirement
- **Follow up regularly** — waitlists are frequently lost or compromised
- "The squeaky mouse gets his bread buttered"

### Buy a boat with a transferable slip
- **This is the primary strategy.** Many sellers advertise "with slip" because they know the slip adds enormous value.
- A $30k boat with a slip may be a better deal than a $15k boat without one
- Ask about liveaboard permission transfer specifically — not all slip transfers include this

### Use a yacht broker
- Brokers know about openings before they're public
- Some marinas give preference to boats coming through established brokers
- A broker who knows your budget ($100k total) and liveaboard intentions can match you with boat+slip packages that never hit open listings

### Yacht club membership
- San Diego Yacht Club, Southwestern YC, Coronado YC, others
- Some have their own slips with shorter waitlists (membership fee filters demand)
- Social network: members know who's leaving, which marinas have openings
- Note: initiation fees and dues add up

### The Log Newspaper
- Publishes annual guide to all west coast slips
- Weekly ads for available slips
- Worth subscribing to during search phase

## Marina Evaluation Checklist

When visiting marinas in person:

- [ ] Shore power quality and availability
- [ ] Pump-out facilities (San Diego Bay is no-discharge)
- [ ] Laundry and shower facilities
- [ ] Mail service / package delivery
- [ ] Parking (included or extra?)
- [ ] Security (gated docks, cameras, lighting)
- [ ] Liveaboard community — talk to existing liveaboards
- [ ] DIY work policy (some marinas prohibit working on your own boat)
- [ ] Proximity to boatyards for haul-outs
- [ ] Proximity to chandleries (West Marine, etc.)
- [ ] Internet infrastructure (for Starlink mounting, shore-based backup)
- [ ] General vibe — is this a place you want to live?
